## WHAT YOU WILL HAVE WHEN DONE
- A real Android APK on your Nothing Phone 1
- "Hey Jarvis" wake word — always listening in background
- South African English accent recognition (en-ZA)
- Full Claude AI intelligence for answers and conversation
- Jarvis personality — dry wit, addresses you by name
- Auto-starts when you reboot your phone
- 100% free — no Picovoice, no third-party accounts except Claude


## HOW THE WAKE WORD WORKS (no special engine needed)
Jarvis uses Android's built-in speech recognition in a continuous loop.
It listens quietly in the background, scans everything it hears for
"Hey Jarvis", and only activates when it hears that phrase.
Your phone vibrates briefly when it activates.


## STEP 1 — GET YOUR CLAUDE API KEY (3 minutes)

1. Go to: https://console.anthropic.com
2. Sign up / log in
3. Click "API Keys" in the left menu
4. Click "Create Key" — copy the key (starts with sk-ant-)
5. Keep it safe — you only see it once

Cost: Less than R15/month at normal usage. Pay-as-you-go, no subscription.


## STEP 2 — GITHUB ACCOUNT (skip if you have one)

1. Go to: https://github.com
2. Click "Sign up" — it's free


## STEP 3 — UPDATE YOUR GITHUB REPO

You already have a repo from before. You need to replace the files
with this new version (which has no Picovoice code).

Option A — Easiest:
1. Delete your old repo (Settings → Danger Zone → Delete repository)
2. Create a new one at https://github.com/new named: jarvis-assistant
3. Upload the contents of this jarvis-android folder (not the folder itself)
4. Commit changes

Option B — Update in place:
1. Open your existing repo
2. Delete the old files one by one and upload the new ones
(Option A is faster)


## STEP 4 — ADD YOUR SECRET (1 minute)

Only ONE secret needed this time (no Picovoice key required).

1. In your GitHub repo → Settings → Secrets and variables → Actions
2. Click "New repository secret"
3. Name:  CLAUDE_API_KEY
   Value: (paste your Claude key)
4. Click "Add secret"


## STEP 5 — BUILD THE APK

1. Click the "Actions" tab in your repo
2. Click "Build Jarvis APK"
3. Click "Run workflow" → "Run workflow"
4. Wait 3-5 minutes for the green tick
5. Click the completed run → scroll to Artifacts
6. Download "Jarvis-Assistant-APK" and unzip it


## STEP 6 — INSTALL ON YOUR NOTHING PHONE 1

Enable unknown sources:
1. Settings → Apps → Special app access → Install unknown apps
2. Find Chrome or Files → toggle ON "Allow from this source"

Install:
1. Transfer app-debug.apk to your phone (email, USB, or Google Drive)
2. Open the file on your phone → tap Install
3. Open "J.A.R.V.I.S" from your app drawer


## STEP 7 — CONFIGURE JARVIS

On first launch:
1. Grant microphone permission when prompted
2. Tap the settings gear (top right)
3. Paste your Claude API key
4. Enter your name (Jarvis will use this)
5. Tap "Save Configuration"
6. Tap the power button — Jarvis comes online


## USING JARVIS

Say "Hey Jarvis" — your phone vibrates to confirm it heard you.
Then speak your command naturally.

Examples:
- "Hey Jarvis, what's the rand dollar exchange rate today?"
- "Hey Jarvis, draft a professional email declining a meeting"
- "Hey Jarvis, what's the weather in Johannesburg?"
- "Hey Jarvis, explain what compound interest means"
- "Hey Jarvis, tell me a joke"

You can also speak the command in the same breath:
- "Hey Jarvis what time is it in London?"


## CRITICAL — BATTERY OPTIMISATION (Nothing Phone 1)

Nothing OS aggressively kills background apps. This MUST be done
or Jarvis will stop working after a few minutes in background.

1. Settings → Battery → Background app management
2. Find J.A.R.V.I.S
3. Set to "No restrictions"

This is the most important step for always-on behaviour.


## TROUBLESHOOTING

Problem: Jarvis doesn't respond to "Hey Jarvis"
Fix: Make sure the app is powered ON (cyan ring).
     Speak clearly. Try "Hey Jarvis" with a slight pause.
     Check battery optimisation above.

Problem: "No Claude API key configured"
Fix: Settings gear → paste your key → Save

Problem: Mishearing your SA accent
Fix: "Optimise for SA English" must be ON in Settings.
     Google's en-ZA speech model handles SA accents well.

Problem: Build failed in GitHub Actions
Fix: Secret name must be exactly: CLAUDE_API_KEY (no spaces, caps)

Problem: App stops after screen off
Fix: Battery optimisation — see above. Also:
     Settings → Display → Always-on display (turn off to save battery)


## INTERNET REQUIREMENT
Wake word detection works offline (it's on-device).
Claude responses require internet. Jarvis will tell you if it can't connect.
